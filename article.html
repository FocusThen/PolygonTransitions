Next step: animating

Now that we have grid, we can use GLSL and vertex shader to animate those triangles. I advise you to read more about shaders and GLSL in "The Book Of Shaders".

The good news is that we can animate each triangle separately, we can even animate each of his vertices on their own!

In code that looks like:

vec3 newPosition = move(position, progress);

Where move function moves and rotates default position with the change of progress value. That could be simple shift on X-axis, for example:

vec3 newPosition = position + progress*vec3(1.,0.,0.);

Or anything you could imagine doing with numbers.
I had a lot of fun experimenting with those effects, hope you will like them. And the possibilities here are endless of course.
